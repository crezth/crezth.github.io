# Character Creation Walkthrough

> **Sources:** Core pp. 32–35; Comp pp. 3–65

A complete step-by-step guide to discovering your Wanderer.

---

## Before You Begin

You are not creating a hero. You are finding a **Wanderer** — someone already alive in the world of Hingsajagra. A farmer who lost everything, a factory worker without a job, a swordmaker who can't make swords, an orphan scarred by occult martial arts sects. You are the Wretched of the Earth.

**You will need:** 2d6, a character sheet, and this guide.

---

## Step 1 — Name, Culture, Truth, and Alignment
*Core p. 32; Comp pp. 3–37*

### Name
Make up a name using common syllables of the setting. You can roll on the Names table (Compendium p.38) by rolling 2d6 twice to get syllables, then combining 1-4 syllables together. Modify freely — add consonants, change vowels.

### Culture (d66 — roll two d6s, read as two-digit number)
Your homeland or chosen culture. There are 36 cultures in the Utter Islands. See [Cultures](14-cultures.md) for the full list. Your culture is a social choice — it shapes your upbringing but does not define you.

**Examples:** Selorongian (center of the world, final bastion of communism), Shennin (the Middle Kingdom, socialist society), Amatsunese (ultranationalist feudal empire), Ragaan (river traders of northern Jeng).

### Truth (d66)
Your paradigm — what you believe is real. This is deeper than religion; it's how you perceive reality itself. See [Truths & Alignments](15-truths-and-alignments.md).

**Examples:** The Material (nothing beyond what we can see), Omniscient Law (Noble Truths of Suffering), Rutraism (all is the Supreme Soul), Demonism (overthrow the divine aristocrats).

### Alignment (2d6, add together)
Your political tendency. Dynamic — it can change through play.

| Roll | Alignment |
|---|---|
| 2 | Fascism |
| 3 | Nationalism |
| 4 | Monarchism |
| 5 | Corporatism |
| 6 | Centrism |
| 7 | Communism |
| 8 | Anarchism |
| 9 | Socialism |
| 10 | Social Democracy |
| 11 | Liberalism |
| 12 | Neoliberalism |

## Step 2 — Attributes and Traits

### Distribute Attributes
You have six scores to assign: **3, 3, 2, 2, 1, 1**. Place them among the six Attributes. None can go above +6.

| Attribute | Governs | Roll When You... |
|---|---|---|
| **Strength** | Adamance, Violence, Labor | Build, lift, break, endure |
| **Skill** | Coordination, Movement, Dexterity | Aim, dodge, steal, move unseen |
| **Mind** | Dialectics, Composure, Rhetoric | Deduce, focus, reason, remember |
| **Wits** | Perception, Reaction Speed, Deception | React, perceive, lie, read intent |
| **Magnetism** | Charisma, Bleeding Heart, Killing Intent | Inspire, persuade, connect heart-to-heart |
| **Psyche** | Magick, Wisdom, Communion | Feel the unseen, regulate emotions, exude will |

**Tips for attribute placement:**
- Melee fighters want **Strength** (damage, Parry) and/or **Skill** (accuracy, Mobility, Dodge)
- Ranged fighters want **Skill** (bows, crossbows, launchers) or **Wits** (guns)
- Casters want **Mind** (attack magick accuracy) and **Psyche** (Sidhi, Resolve, healing)
- Social characters want **Magnetism** (persuasion, charisma) and **Wits** (deception, perception)
- Remember: your Attribute determines which **Defense** stat benefits — plan accordingly

### Choose 1 Trait
Pick or roll (d66) from the [Traits](19-traits.md) list. Traits provide small bonuses/penalties and flavor. They are dynamic and can change through play.

**Popular starting picks:**
- **Adamance** (+4 Health) — for survivability
- **Unblocked Meridians** (half weight, +2 Mobility) — for agility
- **Beast Grip** (one-hand Doublehand weapons, needs Str 3+) — for heavy weapon fighters
- **High Revolutionist** (+1 Mind, +1 Psyche, -2 Mag) — for dedicated revolutionaries
- **Third Eye** (+1 Psyche, -1 Wits, always see Spirits) — for mystics

## Step 3 — Cultivation Dialectic and Esoteric Arts

### Choose a Cultivation Dialectic
Your path of spiritual growth. This determines how you cultivate, what Attainments you gain at each level, and which Attribute gets boosted during Samadhi. Choose one of the four **Left Hand Dialectics** — paths outlawed by the Heavenly Bourgeoisie that Wanderers walk as outsiders. See [Dialectics — Detailed](04b-dialectics-detail.md) for full rules.

| Dialectic | Focus | Cultivation Source | Samadhi Boost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Revolution** | Mystic Materialism, collective | 100mag per enemy in Liquid Steel Ultraviolence | +1 Strength, allies in 5hx get Resist 1 |
| **Internal Elixir** | Self-Cauldron, solo perfection | Double mag from drinking elixirs | +1 Psyche, Strain reduced one tier |
| **Luminous Mind** | Non-Self, meditation | 500mag/month from ritual Samadhi | +1 Mind, +2 all Defenses |
| **Communion** | Gods and Kings, reverence | 100mag/day from shrine offerings | +1 Magnetism, attacks gain Holy, emit light |

*(A fifth category — **Right Hand Dialectics**, system-compliant paths — exists but isn't enumerated in the current rules. Wanderers don't walk it.)*

### Choose 1 Starting Esoteric Art
This is either a **Magick** or a **Technick**. It's your first supernatural ability.

- **Technick** = A martial technique from an Esoteric Martial Art (see [Martial Arts](17-martial-arts.md)). You must pick the *first* Technick of an Art (you learn them in order). Your choice of weapon pattern determines which Arts are available.
- **Magick** = A spell from a Mystic Art school (see [Mystic Arts](18-mystic-arts.md)). These can be learned in any order. You need both hands free (for mudras) and voice (for mantras) to cast.

**If you wield a sword**, consider: Adamantine Lightning Strikes (two quick attacks) or Negation Cut (delayed double damage).

**If you're a brawler**, consider: Vajra Guard (auto-parry) or Vajra Strike (elemental fists).

**If you're a caster**, consider: Wandering Calamity Wraith (deployable bomb), Lotus Balm (healing), or Ghost Hand Spell (telekinesis).

### Define Your Cultivation Womb
Describe two things:
1. **Symbol** — What does your Cultivation Womb look like? A burning lotus? A coiling serpent? A cracked bell?
2. **Color** — What color is your Sidhi when it manifests?

This is purely narrative but becomes important during Tribulations and when using powerful Arts.

## Step 4 — Starting Sundry

### Roll Once on the Item List (d66)
See the Items section in [Weapons & Equipment](16-weapons-and-equipment.md). This gives you one random item to start with.

### Choose Up to 2 Weapons
Total cost must not exceed **300mag**. Consult the weapon list.

**Budget picks:**
- Sundang (saber, 30mag) + Longstick (bludgeon, reach, 2mag) = 32mag
- Kiem (sword, 45mag) + Rectangle Shield (bludgeon, shield, 30mag) = 75mag
- Rattan Stick (bludgeon, can use Skill, 8mag) + Push Dagger (dagger, piercing, concealable, 15mag) = 23mag
- Barong (saber, brutal, ferocious, 100mag) alone = 100mag

**If you picked a ranged weapon** (bow, crossbow, gun), you also gain 2d6 of its basic ammo.

### Choose Starting Attire
Pick **1 Chest Attire** and **1 Leg Attire** from the attire lists. Each gives a small stat bonus and penalty.

**Cheap reliable picks:**
- Chest: Linen Jacket (+1 Str, -1 Mind, 80mag) or Ritual Garb (+1 Psyche, -1 Mind, 200mag)
- Legs: Worker's Pants (+1 Str, -1 Mag, 500mag) or Silk Trousers (+1 Mag, -1 Str, +1 carry, 50mag)

## Step 5 — Calculate Starting Stats
*Core p. 33*

Fill in your character sheet with these derived values:

| Stat | Formula | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Cultivation Level** | 0 | You start as a Mortal |
| **Sidhi Strength** | 3 × Realm (= 3) | Your Strain threshold before it eats your life |
| **Health** | 12 + CL + higher of Str/Psyche | Your hit points. At half = Bloody. At -Bloody = dead. |
| **Carry** | 8 | Add bags for more. Max 2 bags. |
| **Mobility** | 3 + Skill | Hexes you can cross per Action |
| **Clarity** | 0 | Grows through discovery |
| **Magnetite** | 3d6 mag | Your starting cash |
| **Memory** | 6 | How many Magicks you can ready |
| **Parry** | 6 + higher of Str/Skill | Melee defense |
| **Dodge** | 6 + higher of Mind/Wits | Universal defense |
| **Resolve** | 6 + higher of Mag/Psyche | Mental/magick defense |

### Example Calculation
A Wanderer with Str 3, Skill 2, Mind 2, Wits 3, Mag 1, Psyche 1:
- Health = 12 + 0 + 3 (Str) = **15**
- Mobility = 3 + 2 (Skill) = **5**
- Parry = 6 + 3 (Str) = **9**
- Dodge = 6 + 3 (Wits) = **9**
- Resolve = 6 + 1 (Mag or Psyche) = **7**
- Sidhi Strength = 3 × 1 (Realm 1) = **3**

## Step 6 — Final Details
*Core pp. 62, 74*

### Write Your Backstory (briefly)
You don't need a novel. A sentence or two:
- Where are you from?
- What did you lose?
- Why are you wandering?

### The Antinomian Rite
At some point in play (possibly before the game starts, possibly during), your Wanderer must undergo the **Antinomian Rite** to unlock Cultivation. This is a transgressive, subversive act that rips your body out of the Five-Armed Demiurge Array. Work with your Intercessor on what this was or will be.

### Remember
- **You are no hero.** Not until the People call you one.
- **You start off weak.** Don't be afraid to flee or run.
- **Not all fights need pure strength.** Creativity wins.
- **The Masses are you.** Work together or die apart.

## Optional: Cultivation Level 3 Start
*Core p. 33*

For more established characters, your Intercessor may allow a CL 3 start:
1. Create a Wanderer normally (steps 1-5 above)
2. Level up 3 times (+1 to two Attributes each level, +1 Trait each level)
3. Learn 6 Esoteric Arts total (Technicks and/or Magicks)
4. Recalculate all derived stats at CL 3

This represents a Lightning Enterer who has already undergone the Antinomian Rite and begun their journey.
