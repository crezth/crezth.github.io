# 10,000 Swords Against Heaven — System Overview

**Version:** 1.7  
**Author:** JK Saavedra  
**Genre:** Ultraviolent Tropical Gothic Occult Xianxia Role-Playing  
**Dice:** 2d6 only (the proletarian d6)

## What Is This Game?

10,000 Swords Against Heaven (10kSwords) is a spirit cultivation tabletop RPG set in **Hingsajagra**, a dead world reanimated by neoliberalism and the God named Capital. Players are **Wanderers** — common people from the Masses who have begun to Cultivate their spiritual power (Sidhi) and wander the world seeking justice, revolution, and Enlightenment.

## Core Identity — Four Pillars

1. **Ultraviolent** — Violence is the realest language of Hingsajagra. The game deals with breaking free from cycles of violence through the wielding of pure, unalloyed Violence against oppression.
2. **Tropical Gothic** — Centers cultures, aesthetics, and ideologies of the third world, neocolonies, and semicolonies. Gothic horror blended with tropical realities. 1800s-1900s Asia as aesthetic baseline.
3. **Occult Xianxia** — Wanderers cultivate their spirit through pseudoscientific/Dark means. Communities create forces powerful enough to challenge the neoliberal world order.
4. **Dark Fantasy** — Rooted in Buddhism, Daoism, and Folk Religion.

## Player Roles

- **Wanderers** (most players): The PCs. Common people who Cultivate. Not heroes — yet. 4 is a good party size.
- **Intercessor** (1 player): The GM equivalent. A spiritworker and shaman who speaks to The World. Arbitrates, imposes laws, facilitates conversation. Weapons are Telegraph and Consequence.

## Core Play Loop: Telegraph → Action → Consequence

1. **Intercessor Telegraphs** — "The World is like this. This happens to you. What do you do?"
2. **Wanderers declare Actions** — "This is how I respond. This is how I change the World."
3. **Intercessor reveals Consequences** — "This is how the World changes." This becomes the new Telegraph.

## Design Negations (Anti-Principles)

- You are not writing a story — story arises from play
- No demiurges — change what you want to fit your table
- No bigotry — prejudice is the tool of the bourgeoisie
- No fractions — always round down
- Immersion over perfection — the world comes first
- Intercessor must be impartial — reveal the world, don't bend it
