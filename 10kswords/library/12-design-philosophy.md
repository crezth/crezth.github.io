# Design Philosophy & Design Notes

> **Sources:** Core pp. 88–91

## On Immersion
*Core p. 88*

Use the table as you will. The game reaches out to you — meet it. In Lacan's terms, it is the symbolic order for the *real* of your Play. The game wields **negative mechanics** — things it doesn't spell out *must play out* in the fiction, in the immersion.

## On Communism
*Core p. 88*

The game is built from Filipino Masses-oriented Marxist-Leninist-Maoist thought. But MLM here is focused on **materialism** — anti-ideology, focused on the wisdom of the masses.

- No "good" or "evil" faction. Morality is decided by the unfurling lotus of history.
- Liberalism is written for what it is. Fascism for what it is. Communism for what it is.
- The only thing romanticized is the possibility of a better world.
- It realizes the struggle of the colonized and subaltern.
- It acknowledges alternative sciences and medicines of colonized peoples.
- **The skills are for helping each other. LSUV is for fights between decently sized groups. Organized groups.**
- It subsumes religion, not dismisses it. All Religion is Social Fantasy to explain something Real.

## On Emptiness
*Core p. 89*

Violence is nothing but another name for *Sunyata*, Void. The ultimate negation — relentless unceasing violence against everything that exists, has existed, or will ever exist.

## On Ultraviolence (Walter Benjamin Framework)
*Core pp. 89–91*

There are **Violences** and there are **Ultraviolences**.

### Violences
The normal understanding. Crude brutalizations, but also: CEO hiring private armies, communities displaced for malls, priests taking bribes, politicians serving the Ruling Class. **Law-Making and Law-Keeping** violences that perpetuate social orders and cover up Reality.

### Ultraviolences
**Real violence.** Pure violences. Violence that arises only when there is no other choice.

- Does not turn the Wheel of Law-Making and Law-Preserving
- Is destructive, annihilative, and atoning
- As it is pure, it makes the violencer pure
- As it destroys the object, it destroys the subject
- The colonized committing ultraviolence must destroy the institution of Colonialism — destroying the colonizer *and* the colonized
- The Oppressed Class committing ultraviolence must destroy the institution of Oppression — destroying the Ruling Class *and* the Oppressed Class
- It is the complete and utter abolishment, the Negation that creates a new Subject
- Only in annihilation can the Wheel be broken

## Key Design Principles Summarized
*Core pp. 10, 88–91*

1. **Story arises from play** — you are not writing a story
2. **No fractions** — always round down
3. **Immersion over perfection** — the world comes first, balance second
4. **Rulings over rules** — contextual application aligned with Communist ethos
5. **The more radical, the more Real** — reward boldness and creativity
6. **Negative mechanics** — things not spelled out must play out in fiction
7. **Everyone is a Buddha** — treat all World Beings as real people
8. **The People are Immortal** — death is not the end, the community persists
