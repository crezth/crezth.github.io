# Liquid Steel Ultraviolence (LSUV) — Combat System

## What Is LSUV?

The combat system of 10kSwords. Named for its three qualities:

- **Liquid** — No turn order, no action economy, no initiative roll. A tick is a flurry of Actions happening all at once. Anyone can Interrupt at any time.
- **Steel** — Skull-on-pavement violence. Gritty silat of The Raid meets high-flying kung fu of Xianxia. Attacks are flurries; interrupts and counters are expected.
- **Ultraviolence** — Models the thunderbolt violence of kung fu warriors, xianxia cultivators, and silat immortals. Violence is the ultimate answer to the Oppressor.

## When To Use LSUV

Only when violence is necessary for the fiction unfolding. Most violence can be resolved with Checks. LSUV is the structured system for when blood and steel chaos needs to unfurl.

## The Tick

Time breaks into hyperviolent ticks — **3 seconds each**.

**Every combatant has 3 Actions per tick.**

The first to move is whoever has the highest intent to kill. They take the first **Action**.

## Actions

When you take an Action, you can do **anything**. No action lists. Examples:
- Climb, grab, take hold, throw a rope, chug a potion, flip over a table
- Chant mantras, perform mudras, light incense, make offerings
- Seek enlightenment, commit violence, catch your breath
- Run, declare unrequited love, lie down and wait to die

### Movement
- **Moving:** As an Action, move up to your Mobility
- **Stepping:** Before or after an Action, take a free 1hx step

### After An Action
You can keep going and take your next Action if you still have some. You can be interrupted at any point. If you choose not to take an Action or have none left, someone from the enemy side may take one.

## Interrupt

During someone's Action, any being with Actions left can **Interrupt** — doing something immediately. This costs one of their own Actions.

- The Interrupt happens *before* the original Action
- An Interrupt can itself be Interrupted (2nd Interrupt → 1st Interrupt → Original Action)
- Max 2 Interrupts deep

Uses:
- Interpose yourself between ally and attacker
- Attack an enemy's weapon hand mid-swing
- Apply a magick barrier as someone is hit
- **Major drawback:** You lose that Action afterwards

## The Wheel Turns

After all Actions of all combatants are taken, the tick ends and a new tick begins. Everyone refreshes their Actions.

**If a World Being took their Action last in the previous tick, the next tick begins with a Wanderer.**

## Attacks

**Attack Check = 2d6 + Accuracy (Weapon Attribute + Adjustments) vs target's Parry or Dodge**

- Attacker chooses which of their two readied weapons to use
- Target must choose which Defense applies (some Defenses can't apply to certain attacks)
- **Hit (roll ≥ Defense):** Deal damage, apply on-hit effects
- **Miss (roll < Defense):** Target Parries, Dodges, or stands Resolute
- **Critical Hit (double 6s):** Auto-hit, go up two damage tiers, often enter Samadhi
- **Feint:** On hit, instead of damage, lower target's Defense against next Attack by 2

## Attack Adjustments

| Situation | Modifier |
|---|---|
| Flanking (ally adjacent to target) | +1 |
| Cleave (hitting multiple targets) | -1 per target |
| Cover (target behind cover) | -2 |
| Concealed (fog, smoke, darkness) | -2 |
| Called Shot (specific body part) | -2 to -10 |
| Out of Range | -2 |
| Surprise Attack | +2 |
| High Ground (+1hx elevation) | +1 |
| Dual Attack (both weapons at once) | -5 (unless trained) |

## Health & Armor

**Health = 12 + higher of Strength or Psyche + CL**

- **Bloody:** At half Health or lower — triggers certain abilities
- **Death:** Falling to -X Health (X = Bloody value) kills you

**Armor** reduces damage suffered 1-for-1. Cannot bring an attack below 0 damage (minimum 1 on hit).

## Damage

Minimum 1 damage on any hit. Damage cleaves at Health, 1-for-1.

### Extra Damage
+1d6 damage. Multiple instances: roll multiple d6s, only add the highest.

### Strengthened
Increases Damage Tier by 1.

### Resist X
Reduces damage of type X by the resist value, even against armor-ignoring types.

## Environment

The battlefield has three aspects: **objects**, **terrain**, and **elevations**.

### Terrain Types
- **Difficult** — 2hx cost per 1hx moved
- **Bladed** — Minor Piercing damage per 1hx
- **Burning** — Minor Fire damage per 1hx
- **Hazardous** — Minor Ultima damage per 1hx
- **Blocking** — Cannot enter or move through, blocks line of sight
- **Chasm** — Fall to certain doom
- **Hindering** — Can move through but doesn't block line of sight

### Zones (1+ hex areas with persistent effects)
- **Smoke** — Blocks line of sight
- **Healing Spring** — Heal 2 Health at turn start
- **Quieting Field** — Cannot use Arts or sense Sidhi
- **Ultragravity Zone** — Mobility halved, ranged attacks -2

### Field Objects
- **Low Wall** (5 DR) — Cover, +1 Mobility cost
- **Blasting Barrel** (3 Health) — Burst 1, 6 fire damage on destruction
- **Breaking Bridge** (8 DR) — Removes movement over chasm
- **Shrine** (8 DR) — Pray for +1 all Defenses; destroying side gets -1 all Defenses
- **Swinging Chain** (5 DR) — Skill Check to swing up to 10hx

### Elevation
1hx+ higher = elevated. Range = max of horizontal or vertical distance.

### Force-Move
Push, pull, knockback. Push/knockback = away from you. Pull = toward. Force-move = any direction. Cannot force-move up or down.

### Collision
Force-moved into terrain/object/person: suffer DMG = remaining hexes of movement. The thing you hit also suffers the same DMG.

### Line of Sight
Straight line from your hex to target. Cover/Blocking terrain gives defense bonuses. Full intersection with Blocking terrain = no LoS.

### Size
- **Average:** 1hx wide, 2hx tall
- **Large:** 3hx wide, 5hx tall
- **Huge:** 5hx wide, 7hx tall

## Death In Combat

**Falling to -X Health kills you** (X = Bloody value).

When you die:
- Your Cultivation leaves the world
- New Wanderer can be discovered from a Community where you performed a Great Work
- Can inherit same Cultivations/Skills or respec (no more than previous Wanderer had)
- **The People are Immortal** — but don't abuse the Community's resources

## Intercessor Combat Tips

- **Flatten Choice** for World Beings — don't model them as tactical geniuses. Let them take 3 simple actions that Wanderers can cut into.
- Establish what each side **Wants**. Fight until one side gets it or there's nothing to fight for.
- Maps are optional — for visual reference, not strict measurement.
